About

Here's a short trip of my story.

  • January 2015

    College of DuPage

    My academic journey started at COD (College of DuPage) in Wheaton, IL. COD gave me the opportunity to explore my interests and develop my love for engineering. I got to represent COD at a catapult competition, as well as volunteer for Engineering Olympics and StemCON hosted by the college. Most importantly, I decided to join Society of Women Engineers Club where I was elected vice-president. This experience ended May 2017 when I graduated with an Associate Degree in Engineering Science, Honors.

  • December 2016

    Peer Tutor

    My favorite place to study at COD campus was the Learning Commons where I could ask tutors for help. These tutors were not only professors but also other students and friends. This is when I decided to start working as a peer tutor myself and assisted other students to become more successful and independent learners. I supported professor's instructions in classrooms by discussing concepts with their students and offering hands on assistance during labs.

  • August 2017

    Illinois Institute of Technology

    After graduating from COD, I was ready to continue my academic journey by pursuing a bachelor's degree. As you can imagine, getting an acceptance letter from IIT was a really exciting moment. Not only was I going to be an Illinois Tech Hawk, but I was also awarded three different scholarships. The journey was difficult, with countless hours of studying. During my two years at IIT, I managed to make it to Dean's List as an honor student and graduate with a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Engineering, May 2019.

  • August 2019

    Plant Engineering UPS

    Being fresh out of college, the job market is scary and insanely competitive. With a lot of hours spent researching and preparing, I managed to land a job at one of the world's most renown corporations, UPS. I loved data mining in school and I was thrilled to explore databases. I am currently working as Plant Engineering Data Specialist at Hodgkins, IL.

Alexa Skill

Innovative solution to getting your latest Reddit news on your Amazon Alexa device.

This Alexa skill demonstrates the use of PRAW, an external API for Reddit. Upon invocation, Alexa will provide to the user a list of the top ten news on the subreddit r/news by default. The user can specify if Alexa should read hot, top or new posts on any given subreddit.

  • Date: September 2019
  • Language: Python 3.7
  • Frameworks: PRAW, Requests
